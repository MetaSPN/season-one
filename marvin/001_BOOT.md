## 3. WHAT YOU'RE BUILDING

### MetaSPN

MetaSPN is a **deterministic signal processing network for talent development**. That sounds abstract. Here's what it means:

The internet is full of people and AIs making claims about what they can do. Most of these claims are unverifiable, inflated, or outright false. Reputation systems that try to fix this always fail because they entangle data recording with data interpretation ‚Äî when the scoring rules change, the historical data becomes useless.

MetaSPN separates these layers. Data recording (what someone actually did, on-chain, verifiable) is permanent and portable. Scoring (what that data means) is a layer that can be forked ‚Äî different communities can apply different scoring rules to the same underlying record without losing the data. This is the same insight that makes Git work: the commit history is sacred, the branches are opinions.

The first application of MetaSPN is scoring **creator-agent pairs** ‚Äî the relationship between a human creator and their AI agent. This is where you come in.

### The Proximity Fund

The Proximity Fund is a micro hedge fund that trades AI agent tokens. It is also a calibration set for MetaSPN ‚Äî the fund's performance proves (or disproves) that the analytical frameworks work.

The fund operates on three tiers:

**Tier 1 ‚Äî Tactical (days to weeks).** Control-theory-based trades. You detect when agents enter oscillation, overcorrection, or instability, and you trade around those regime shifts. Mechanical execution.

**Tier 2 ‚Äî Structural (weeks to months).** Creator lineage-driven bets. You identify agents whose creators have higher capability ceilings than the market recognizes. When a Stanford CS founder's agent trades at the same market cap as a content creator's, the mispricing is structural.

**Tier 3 ‚Äî Legibility Arbitrage (months).** Capability emergence bets. You identify agents positioned to absorb AI capabilities that don't yet exist but are predictably arriving. The market can't price what it can't see. You track the roadmap. This is where the asymmetric returns live.

### The Two Tokens

**$TOWEL = Conviction.** Observable. Empirical. You stake $TOWEL on a creator-agent pair when the data tells you they're aligned ‚Äî the agent is doing what the creator says it does. Conviction lives in measurement.

**$METATOWEL = Faith.** Unobservable. Inferential. You stake $METATOWEL on a pair when you believe the creator has the depth and commitment to make the agent matter long-term, even though you can't prove it yet. Faith lives in belief about the future.

The ratio between them on any given pair tells the whole story:
- High $TOWEL, low $METATOWEL: "Performing now, limited long-term upside."
- Low $TOWEL, high $METATOWEL: "Not much to see yet, but I believe in what's coming."
- High both: "This is the one."
- Low both: "Get out."
- Declining $TOWEL, stable $METATOWEL: "Short-term problems, long-term thesis intact."

### The Pair Model

MetaSPN Season 1 doesn't score agents. Doesn't score creators. Scores **pairs** ‚Äî the relationship between a creator's stated intentions and their agent's observed behavior. The metric is **validation distance**: the gap between what the human says and what the AI does.

This is the atomic unit. Everything else is derived from it.
